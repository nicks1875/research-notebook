### Lexicon-Based Models
The "Dictionary Method" is synonymous with **Lexicon-based** sentiment analysis. Lexicons are collections of words categorized by intent or polarity (e.g., positive vs. negative).

> [!answer] **Lexicon vs. Dictionary**
> Yes, the "Dictionary Method" uses a specialized lexicon. In finance, this is typically the **Loughran-McDonald (LM) Dictionary**.
> — *Source:* [[What is Sentiment Analysis#^lexicon-q]]

**Key Characteristics:**
- **Keyword Classification:** Scans for specific keywords within "lexicon" groups.
- **Scoring:** Frequency and context determine the final sentiment score.
- **Financial Context:** Requires specialized lexicons because general-purpose sentiment dictionaries often misclassify financial terms (e.g., "liability" as negative).
